**Length:** ~1,400 chars. Hook fits in 140 chars.

---

```
Tuesday, 2:47pm.

You have a 1:1 with Marcus in 13 minutes.

His prior notes are somewhere in Slack. The KPI he was working on
is in a spreadsheet you haven't opened since Thursday. The task he
flagged on Monday: you remember the gist, not the specifics.

You'll wing it. He'll wing it. Both of you will leave thinking it
was fine.

This is what most management feels like for the people doing it
well enough to keep their team together but not well enough to
actually move them forward.

It's not a focus problem. Or a discipline problem. Or a
"you should journal more" problem.

It's a rhythm problem.

The information about your team lives in eight different places.
Your week resets every Monday from scratch. The work of being a
good manager keeps colliding with the work of being a manager
at all.

Cadence puts the rhythm of your week in one place. One-on-ones
with prior notes already loaded. KPIs alongside the person, not
in a separate tab. Tasks that link back to the conversation that
created them.

Your team has a rhythm. Your job is to keep it.

#Management #Leadership #PeopleManagement #TeamManagement
```
