Search + filters at the top. Featured post + 3 recents side by side. Then horizontal sections per category ("Latest in 1:1s", "Latest in Delegation"). Resource section at bottom. Like HubSpot or Help Scout. Feels rich and editorial.

B — Search-First Grid

Big search bar dominates the top. Below it: category pills showing post counts. Then a uniform 3-column grid — every post treated equally, no "featured" hierarchy. Resources section pinned at the bottom as a separate block. Feels fast and functional like Notion or Intercom.

C — Sidebar Tool

Persistent left sidebar with search and category filters (with post counts). Main area shows a scannable list of articles with thumbnails, category, and read time. Most "tool-like" of the three — feels like a knowledge base or app sidebar. Resources section lives in the sidebar as a category.

My recommendation

B is the sweet spot for Cadence right now — prominent search, clean grid with post counts per category, and a dedicated resource section ready to fill in. It's fast to build, SEO-friendly, and feels like a real tool without the complexity of a full magazine layout. A gets more interesting as the post library grows past ~30 articles.
